DeepCQF: Making CQF Scheduling More
Intelligent and Practicable
Zongrong Cheng, Dong Yang*, Weiting Zhang, Jie Ren, Hongchao Wang, and Hongke Zhang
School of Electronic and Information Engineering, Beijing Jiaotong University, China
Abstract—Recently, Cyclic Queuing and Forwarding (CQF)
based shaper has been proposed by IEEE 802.1Qch to satisfy the
demands for deterministic industrial services. However, CQF only
deﬁnes the queuing model and ﬂow features, the investigations of
scheduling for Time-Triggered ﬂows are still at the early stage.
How to make the scheduling more efﬁcient and intelligent is still
an open issue to be solved. In this paper, we propose a Deep
Reinforcement Learning (DRL) assisted two-stage scheduling
algorithm, namely DeepCQF, to solve the temporal scheduling
problem based on CQF model, which can be adopted in the off-
line and on-line scenarios. Due to the high schedulability and
low time cost requirements in the off-line and on-line scenarios,
respectively, DeepCQF uses the DRL algorithm to take immediate
response for the coming ﬂows when scheduling online, while in
the off-line scenario, DeepCQF combines the DRL and heuristic
algorithms together as a two-stage mechanism to further improve
the schedulability. We compare the DeepCQF with other state-
of-the-art algorithms for CQF. Experiment results show that
DeepCQF can increase the number of accommodating ﬂows by
an average of 2 times than Greedy algorithm and the convergence
speed by 3 times at most than Double Deep Q-network algorithm.
Index Terms—Time-Sensitive Networking; CQF Model; Deep
Reinforcement Learning; Temporal Scheduling.
I. INTRODUCTION
In recent years, the demands for deterministic network
services have been penetrated into a large number of domains,
such as industrial automation, automotive control systems,
avionic applications, and mobile backhaul, among others [1],
[2]. However, the traditional methods in packet switched
networks are not suitable for the services of such hard real-
time requirements [3]. In order to cope with the problem,
the IEEE 802.1 TSN working group has developed a set
of promising standards of Time-Sensitive Networking (TSN),
which targets at the deterministic forwarding applications and
bridging at the link layer of the network, providing these
services with deterministic latency, jitter, and high reliability.
TSN provides the per-queue granularity trafﬁc shaping
solutions to deal with the synchronized ﬂow forwarding prob-
lems that include Time Aware Shaper (TAS, a.k.a. IEEE
802.1Qbv) and Cyclic Queuing and Forwarding (CQF, a.k.a.
IEEE 802.1Qch) with minor extensions to standard switches.
TAS is required to dynamically plan the conﬁgurations of
Gate Control List (GCL) to schedule Time-Triggered (TT)
ﬂows before the task starts. However, with the ﬂow features
changing, the recalculation of GCL conﬁgurations can be com-
putational complex. Alternatively, CQF is proposed as an easy-

to-use model by installing the static conﬁgurations on GCL.
By cyclically switching a pair of Ping-Pong queues, CQF guar-
antees the hop-by-hop deterministic bounded latency. Thus, the
latency introduced by ﬂow transmission completely depends
on the time interval size (i.e., cycle time [4]) and the number
of hops, and is unaffected by any changes of topology or
interference from other non-deterministic ﬂows [5].
As IEEE 802.1Qch only provides the queuing model and
ﬂow features, the problem intended for the scheduling of TT
ﬂows is still an open issue to be solved and optimized. Our
original intention of this paper is to explore how to make the
temporal scheduling more intelligent and practicable.
Many algorithms have introduced valuable experience for
scheduling TT ﬂows in TSN networks. Steiner et al. [6]
focused on the synthesized schedules based on TAS by using
the solver of Satisﬁability Modulo Theories (SMT). However,
with the increase of the number of ﬂows and nodes in the
network, the time cost of SMT will be unacceptable. Yan et
al. [7] proposed the Tabu-ITP algorithm based on CQF model
and got a good trade-off between the mapping quality and
computational overhead by regulating the injection time for
each ﬂow. Quan et al. [5] further proposed a light-weight
dynamic algorithm using CQF model to schedule TT ﬂows
online, and it greatly improved the number of scheduled ﬂows.
Nevertheless, traditional algorithms cannot learn intelligently
and actively based on experience, thus it cannot get better over
time. Prados-Garzon et al. [1] presented a Deep Reinforcement
Learning (DRL) solution, LEARNET, to learn scheduling poli-
cies based on asynchronous networks, and it achieved higher
gains of revenue in 5G backhaul environments. Although
DRL has good generalizations for dynamic environments,
it is difﬁcult to be as good as the optimization of exact
solutions intended for the particular scenario. Therefore, we
explore whether we can combine the learning and traditional
algorithms to satisfy the different requirements for scheduling
TT ﬂows ofﬂine and online, and improve the schedulability,
resource utilization, and reduce time cost by using CQF model.
In this paper, we propose a DRL assisted two-stage schedul-
ing algorithm, namely DeepCQF, to schedule the TT ﬂows in
CQF based TSN networks, which can be adopted in the off-
line and on-line scenarios. Speciﬁcally, in the on-line schedul-
ing scenario, making quick decisions for the new coming ﬂows
is required, thus we design a LearnSched (i.e., Learning based
scheduling) algorithm as the ﬁrst stage of DeepCQF to take
the immediate response for each coming ﬂow. In the off-
line scenario, we focus on scheduling more TT ﬂows, and
a TSSched (i.e., Tabu Search based scheduling) algorithm is

To evaluate the performance of our proposed algorithm, we
compare the DeepCQF with other state-of-the-art algorithms
under various system settings. The experiment results show
that DeepCQF can schedule nearly twice the ﬂows than
Greedy algorithm, and increase the number of schedulable
ﬂows by 50%, 40%, and 10% on average compared with SMT,
Double Deep Q-Network (DDQN), and Tabu-ITP (Injection
Time Planning) algorithms, respectively. And it has the higher
temporal resource utilization as well as lower time cost in
the off-line and on-line scenarios, respectively. To make the
scheduling practicable, we deploy the DeepCQF into our TSN
testbed to schedule different TT ﬂows in real environments.
In conclusion, the contributions of this paper are:
• We develop a temporal-scheduling model for the schedul-
ing problem of TT ﬂows based on the CQF queuing mech-
anism and our investigations of the relationship between the
schedulability and temporal resource allocation policy.
• We propose DeepCQF, a DRL assisted algorithm ﬁrstly
used to solve the CQF based scheduling problem of TT ﬂows
in TSN networks under the off-line and on-line scenarios.
• We evaluate the performance of the proposed algorithm
in simulated environments, and validate the practicability of
scheduling by deploying the DeepCQF into our TSN testbed
to schedule different TT ﬂows in real environments.

II, the scheduling problem is formulated by using the CQF

the formulated problem in the off-line and on-line schedul-
ing scenarios. The performance evaluation, followed by the
conclusion are presented in Section IV and V, respectively.

and Q7 opens the RX gate to receive ﬂows from the upstream
devices. In the odd time interval T1, the operations execute
conversely. Furthermore, once the packets are transmitted from

III. DEEPCQF: DRL ASSISTED TWO-STAGE
SCHEDULING ALGORITHM
Compared with the non-learning algorithms, DRL can ac-
tively learn from the dynamic environment and has lower
time cost when testing. Hence, DRL is more preferable to
be applied into the on-line scenario. However, in the off-line
scenario, the results learned from DRL are not easy to be
better than traditional algorithms because the training process
for DRL is too generalized. In order to improve the quality of
results, the heuristic algorithm is added as the second stage of
DeepCQF to schedule the static ﬂows.

TSSched design: In order to further improve the perfor-
mance of scheduling TT ﬂows ofﬂine, we design the TSSched
algorithm to search optimal solutions in speciﬁc static envi-
ronments, as the second stage of DeepCQF. The pseudo code
of TSSched is shown in Algorithm 2.
The high-quality initial results are obtained by LearnSched.
The TT ﬂows are divided into two sets, which are the
successfully scheduled set Fsuc and the set Ffail that is failed
to be scheduled, respectively. The core design of the searching
process is shown from line 7 to 13. In the probability of δ, the
random searching strategy is used to explore better solutions.
Speciﬁcally, in the shifting mode, the randomly selected ﬂows
that belong to Fsuc can be exchanged with each other to
change the distribution of time interval usage, while in the
exchanging mode, one or more ﬂows are randomly selected
from Fsuc, and moved to Ffail. Another searching strategy
refers to the domain expert, which is more inclined to select
the ﬂows with larger size, lower deadline, longer path length
and period to exchange. By iteratively searching the neighbor
candidates, the near-optimal results can be computed.

A. Performance Evaluation in Simulated Environments
The parameter settings are listed as follows:
• Settings for DeepCQF: For LearnSched, we set two
fully connected hidden layers, each with 1000 neurons. The
sizes of replay buffer and sample batch are set to 1e4 and
64, respectively. We use Adam with 0.0001 learning rate and
discounting factor of 0.9 to train the evaluation network param-
eters. For TSSched, the maximum iterations and repetitions are
set to 3000 and 200, respectively, which means there are 200
candidate solutions will be generated in each iteration. The
tabu length is set to 500.
• Settings for TSN network: We set the benchmark
topology with 10 hosts and 2 switches shown in Fig. 4(b). The
link bandwidth is 1000Mb/s, and the queue length is 1024 to
leave enough buffer space. The size of each packet is in the
range of 50B∼1KB as recommended by Industrial Internet
Consortium [11]. And 700 TT ﬂows are scheduled under
various settings of the time interval size and ﬂow periods.
The experiment results are listed as follows:
• Convergence speed comparison: Here we set the time
interval size to 400us. The ﬂows with the set of periods
{1600us, 3200us} are scheduled ofﬂine. The iterative search-
ing process is shown in Fig. 5(a). Compared with the Tabu-ITP
with exchanging mode [7] and DDQN, DeepCQF increases
the convergence speed by nearly three times. This is because
DeepCQF is initialized with the results of LearnSched that
has a better observation of the global network status compared
with greedy and random policies adopted in the initialization
of Tabu-ITP and DDQN, respectively. Therefore, DeepCQF
can be more easy and quick to obtain the optimal solutions.
• Schedulability comparison: We compare the DeepCQF
with the other four state-of-the-art algorithms adopted in
TSN scheduling problems ofﬂine. In Fig. 5(b), DeepCQF

B. Scheduling on TSN Testbed in Real Environments
Implementations on TSN testbed: As shown in Fig. 4(a),
our TSN testbed utilizes the fully centralized architecture ac-
cording to IEEE 802.1Qcc. We build the benchmark topology,
which has 10 hosts and 2 switches, depicted in Fig. 4(b).
Each NIC of the devices is INTEL I210. The algorithm is
implemented in the Centralized Network Conﬁguration (CNC)
of controller with AMD R9 5950X CPU, 32GB of RAM. The
CQF based shaper is implemented by using Trafﬁc Control
(TC) tool executing the queuing discipline of TAPRIO. We use
LinuxPTP to synchronize the system clock. The time interval
size is 400us, and the Ring parameters of TX and RX are 1024.
The TT ﬂows are created from the SAMPLE-APP-TAPRIO
ﬂow generator [12] with the periods {1600us, 3200us}. Taking
50 TT ﬂows with 100,000 packets for scheduling as an
example, the experiment procedures and results are listed:
Step 1: Registration. The TT ﬂows with the random source
and destination addresses are registered by users to Centralized
User Conﬁguration (CUC) and stored in the database.
Step 2: Gathering. CNC gathers the network status through
the Link Layer Discovery Protocol (LLDP).
Step 3: Computing. Based on the ﬂow features and the net-
work status extracted from the database, DeepCQF computes
the temporal resource allocation policy for each ﬂow.
Step 4: Deployment. CNC deploys the GCL and computing
results to the hosts and switches through the Network Conﬁg-
uration Protocol (NETCONF) to schedule the ﬂows.
In Fig. 6(b), the scheduling results show that for the packets
traversing one switch along the path, the average end-to-end
latency is 311.29us with a maximum not exceeding 800us.
For the packets transmitting through two switches, the end-to-
end latency is on an average of 686.28us and the maximum
is not greater than 1200us. The jitter of all the packets is
188.21us on average and is no more than 700.08us. The results

V. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we have developed an algorithm named
DeepCQF to deal with the temporal scheduling problem in
CQF based TSN networks in the off-line and on-line scenarios,
respectively. The experiment results have demonstrated that
the DeepCQF has higher schedulability, temporal resource
utilization, and lower time cost under various system settings.
The practicability of scheduling has been validated by running
different TT ﬂows on our TSN testbed in real environments. In
the future, designing novel mixed ﬂow scheduling algorithms
with CQF model would be an interesting direction.
